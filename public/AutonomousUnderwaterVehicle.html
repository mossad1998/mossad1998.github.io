Autonomous Underwater Vehicle

This project aimed at building autonomous underwater vehicle (AUV) to represent Ain shams university in RoboSub international competition at the U.S. Navy Space and Naval Warfare Systems Center Pacific's TRANSDEC Anechoic pool in San Diego, California. The team consisted of 30 members divided into three groups, I was the leader of electrical engineering group making my way as the youngest group leader in the entire school of engineering. we were proud to be the only team participating from the Middle East.

My repsposibilities inclouded hardware-software interface, and power electronics. Moreover, I was reposible for training new group members by coducting 3 monthes workshop on communication protocols. the output of my group was the electrical kit considered as the heart of the vehicle. the kit is divided into two sides power side and control side. this technique helps in elemenating noise and heat to propagate from power side to control side. power side carries the power distributions system for different ESCs, servos and DC converters. control side carries different MCUs such as arduino and jetson board, sensors, and camers.

AUV accomplished the following missions:
1.Navigate from the dock to release buoys.
2.Pass over an obstacle course.
3.Drop markers on specific locations.
4.Shoot torpedoes on specific colored buoys.
5.Find a pinger using hydrophone, grab an object and move/release the object.
